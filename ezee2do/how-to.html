Getting started

Open the app. The first time you launch it, a quick tour walks you through the basics. You can replay the tour any time from the menu.

There's nothing to sign up for. No account, no password, no email verification. Your lists are already private and ready to go.

Creating lists

You can have as many lists as you want — one for groceries, one for work, one for a trip, one for errands. Each list is independent.

On iPhone/iPad: tap the list name at the top of the screen. The lists menu slides in. Tap New List, give it a name, and you're done.

On Mac: click the list name in the header to open the lists menu, then choose New List…. Or just press ⌘N.

Adding tasks

On iPhone/iPad: tap the "Type a task" field at the top, type, and tap the + button (or press Return on your keyboard).

On Mac: type in the top row and press Return. Done.

There are no due dates, priorities, tags, or attachments by design. Adding a task takes under two seconds, which is the whole point.

Completing and deleting tasks

To mark a task done: tap or click the empty circle next to it. Completed tasks slide to the bottom of the list automatically so you always see what's still outstanding at the top.

To delete a task: on iPhone/iPad, swipe left on the task. On Mac, right-click the task and choose Delete.

To uncheck a task: tap or click the checkmark again. It moves back up to the top section.

Switching between lists

On iPhone/iPad: swipe left or right anywhere in the main area to move between lists. Or tap the list name to open the full lists menu and pick one.

On Mac: click the list name in the header and pick from the dropdown. Or use ⌘[ to go to the previous list and ⌘] for the next one.

Copying tasks and lists

Sometimes you want to share a task or a whole list in another app — Messages, Mail, Notes, a text.

To copy a single task:

  • On iPhone/iPad, long-press the task and choose Copy.
  • On Mac, right-click the task and choose Copy. Or highlight just part of the text and right-click to copy the selection.

To copy the entire list: open the list menu and choose Copy List. Each task becomes one line, ready to paste anywhere.

Syncing between devices

eZee 2do syncs through your own iCloud account — no developer servers, no data we can see.

To turn sync on:

  1. On each device, go to Settings → [your name] → iCloud.
  2. Make sure iCloud Drive is turned on.
  3. Scroll down to the app list and make sure eZee 2do is toggled on.

That's it. Changes you make on your iPhone show up on your Mac (and iPad) within a few seconds when both are online. The app still works offline; changes sync automatically once you're back on the network.

Live Activities (iPhone)

On iPhone, you can turn on Live Activities so your current list shows up on your Lock Screen and in the Dynamic Island (on supported iPhones). You can see your top three outstanding tasks and how many are done without unlocking your phone.

To enable it, open the menu and toggle Live Activities. You can turn it off any time.

Mac tips

Launching from the Dock opens eZee 2do as a regular window that stays until you close it. Use this when you want a workspace that sticks around.

Clicking the checkmark in your menu bar opens eZee 2do as a popover that hides the moment you click outside of it. Use this for quick glances — check the top of your list, add something, move on.

Both behaviors are the same app with the same data. It's just about how you want to interact with it in the moment.

Keyboard shortcuts (Mac)

  • ⌘N — New list
  • ⌘[ — Previous list
  • ⌘] — Next list
  • Return — Add the task you just typed
  • ⌘W — Close the window (app stays running in the menu bar)
  • ⌘Q — Quit eZee 2do
