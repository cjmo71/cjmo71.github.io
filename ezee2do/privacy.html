The short version

eZee 2do is designed with privacy as the default. We don't collect any data. We don't have a server that sees your tasks. Your lists live on your device and (optionally) sync through your personal iCloud account, which only you can access. There are no accounts, no analytics, no trackers, no ads.

What we collect

Nothing. eZee 2do does not collect, transmit, or store any personal information on our servers. We do not use analytics, tracking pixels, advertising identifiers, crash reporting SDKs, or any third-party code that phones home.

Where your data lives

Your lists and tasks are stored in two places, both of which you control:

  1. On your device — in the app's local storage, just like the Notes app or any other first-party Apple app.
  2. Your personal iCloud account — so your lists sync between your iPhone, iPad, and Mac. Your tasks live in a private iCloud Drive folder that belongs to your Apple ID — the same kind of personal space Apple gives every iCloud app. Only you can open it. The developer (that's me, Chris Moore) cannot see inside it, even if you contact support.

If you sign out of iCloud or turn off iCloud for eZee 2do, sync stops. Your data stays on the device you added it on.

Optional: Advanced Data Protection. If you've turned on Apple's Advanced Data Protection for iCloud in your Apple ID settings, your eZee 2do data is end-to-end encrypted in iCloud as well. That means not even Apple can read it — only your signed-in devices can. This is a setting you control on your Apple ID; eZee 2do automatically benefits from it if it's on.

Network access

The app communicates with Apple's iCloud servers to sync your data across your devices. That's it. There are no other network requests, no external APIs, no telemetry. You can verify this yourself by putting your device in Airplane Mode — the app keeps working locally, and there's nothing else it's trying to reach.

Children's privacy

eZee 2do does not knowingly collect data from anyone, including children. The app is rated 4+ and is safe for all ages. Since we don't collect anything at all, there's nothing child-specific to protect against.

Third parties

We do not share, sell, or disclose any data to third parties, because we don't collect any data to begin with. The app does not include any third-party advertising networks, analytics services, or tracking SDKs.

The one third party involved is Apple itself, via iCloud. Apple's handling of iCloud data is covered by the Apple Privacy Policy.

Your rights

Because we don't collect any personal data, there's nothing for us to export, correct, or delete on request — we simply don't have a copy. You have full control at all times:

  • To see your data: open the app.
  • To export your data: use the copy feature to copy a list to your clipboard.
  • To delete your data: delete the tasks, or delete the app. Removing the app also removes local data; disabling iCloud for the app removes cloud copies.

Changes to this policy

If this policy ever changes, we'll update the date at the top of this page. Continued use of the app after a change means you accept the updated policy. Material changes will also be called out in the app's release notes.

Contact

Questions about privacy? Visit the Support page and send a message through the contact form. A real human reads every message.
