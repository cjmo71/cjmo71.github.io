First launch

When you first open Somos InControl, iOS will ask for Local Network permission. Tap Allow — this is how the app discovers your Sonos speakers on your Wi-Fi. Without it, the app has nothing to control.

Once permission is granted, the app scans your network and lists the Sonos speakers it finds. Discovery usually takes a couple of seconds.

A short interactive tour runs automatically the first time. It walks you through the main controls with highlighted callouts. You can skip it and come back to it any time from the side menu.

Picking a speaker

The bar at the bottom of the screen shows tabs for each Sonos speaker discovered on your network. Tap a tab to switch which speaker you're controlling. The rest of the app updates immediately to show that speaker's current volume, EQ, and preset.

If you only have one Sonos device, you'll only see one tab. If you have several, swipe the tab bar if needed and pick the one you want.

Volume

The Volume card is the big control near the top. Drag the slider left or right to lower or raise the volume on the selected speaker. Changes apply immediately — no "Apply" button to tap.

If you want to mute the speaker without changing the slider position, tap the speaker icon next to the slider.

Presets

A preset is a saved combination of volume and EQ settings. Think of it as "set the speaker this way with one tap."

To save a preset:

  1. Dial in the volume, bass, treble, and loudness settings you want.
  2. Open the Presets row and tap + Save as preset.
  3. Give it a name — something like "Movie Night" or "Late TV" works well.
  4. Tap Save.

To recall a preset: just tap its name in the Presets row. All the saved settings apply at once.

To delete a preset: long-press its name and choose Delete.

EQ, Loudness, and TruePlay

The EQ card gives you direct control of the sound on the selected speaker:

  • Bass — boosts or cuts the low end. Negative values reduce bass, positive values add it.
  • Treble — adjusts high frequencies. Negative for warmer, positive for brighter.
  • Loudness — a toggle that applies a gentle EQ curve at low volumes so quiet music still sounds full. Most people leave this on.
  • TruePlay — Sonos's room calibration. If your speaker supports it and you've calibrated it through the official Sonos app, you can toggle TruePlay on and off here. When it's on, the speaker's sound is tuned to the acoustics of the room it's in.

Side menu

Tap the hamburger button in the top-left corner to open the side menu. From here you can:

  • Replay the tutorial
  • Open the "Getting Started" guide
  • View the privacy policy and support page
  • Check the app version

Replaying the tutorial

If you want to see the walkthrough again, open the side menu and tap Replay Tutorial. It runs the full interactive tour with the highlighted callouts just like the first time you opened the app.
